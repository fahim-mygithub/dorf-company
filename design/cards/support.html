Draft direction (2026-07-15). The Support role, same three-layer shape as the Tank sheet: the role is a shared substrate (Communion), each class is one Class Power that channels it, and a skill tree (post-MVP) upgrades that power. Numbers are unsimmed placeholders.

Rev 2026-07-16 — the two stances were reworked. The Bard’s song no longer burns Communion to stay up; it’s held by touching 3 distinct targets a turn (an AoE counts as 1), and comes up short → it breaks. The Druid is rebuilt around deck manipulation: pick 🐻 Bear / 🦅 Hawk / 🐺 Wolf, hand 2 cards back to your deck to pay for it, and get paid — hard — for the card types you draw rather than the ones you play. Both changes pull the same lever: the cost moved out of a meter and into how you play. The Bard pays in reach, the Druid pays in cards.

📿 SUPPORT — the Communion substrate

Communion is the party’s shared reserve of faith and morale. Every support builds it by helping an ally — a heal, a shield, a buff banks 1 📿 — and support cards read off it. The cards build the resource; the Class Power channels it, differently per class: the Cleric runs a passive aura that auto-discharges every 5 casts (heal-all + smite-all, scaled by your cast streak), the Bard spends a lump to strike up a party-wide song, the Druid spends a lump to shift into a beast. (The Cleric’s aura reads cast types directly instead of banking Communion — it’s the always-on member of the trio.)

Communion is the ignition, never the upkeep. It buys the Bard’s song and the Druid’s shift, and then it’s out of the way — neither power drains a meter to stay alive. What holds them up is how you play: the Bard must spread its turn across 3 distinct targets, the Druid must draw the card types its form pays for. That keeps the failure state legible (you can see it in your own hand) instead of hiding it in a bar ticking toward zero.

Communion lives on the party dict, host-side, so both the meter and the power ride the snapshot.

🟣 In combat: the Class Power

Each dwarf carries its Class Power as an orb on its portrait — a Hearthstone-style hero power. Tap your own dwarf to fire it; the orb shows the gate (its cooldown, or the resource it spends). Cards build the resource, the Class Power spends it. In co-op you only ever fire your own.

CLASS POWERWild Shapestance · spend a lump of 📿 · choose 🐻 / 🦅 / 🐺 · costs 2 cards · lasts 3 turns
Spend Communion to shift — and choose the form (a stance, 3 turns). It resolves at the start of your next turn: shuffle 2 cards from your hand back into your deck, and you do not replace them. That’s the real price of the form — a −2 hand, two plays you simply don’t get. You do choose which two, and they go back into the deck, not the discard: you aren’t throwing them away, you’re re-seeding.
It has to buy back a whole turn of tempo, so it pays hard. At the start of every turn you’re shifted, the form reads your hand and pays for every card of its school in it — the three forms are the three schools, one each (see cards/dps.html):
🐻 Bear — every 🛡️ block card in hand → +5 Guard, free.
🐺 Wolf — every ⚔️ physical card in hand → your first attack this turn hits +5 harder.
🦅 Hawk — every 🧿 spell card in hand → +3 block to the whole party, free. (Reads lower per card only because it multiplies by party size.)
The payout is on the draw, not the play: you’re paid for holding the right types, not spending them — so the two you give back are the two your form wasn’t going to pay for anyway. It isn’t a mulligan, it’s a tithe you get to aim.
▸ tap your dwarf's Class Power in combat to fire it
Feeds it: Communion 📿 — nature skills and heals bank it; Wild Shape spends a lump to shift. But the lump isn’t the real price — 2 cards are. A form only ever pays what your deck can draw, so the Druid is the class that rewards a list built lopsided on purpose: a narrow deck is what makes the two you hand back the easy ones, and then the hand pays you before you spend a point of energy.

The build cost — what Support adds

Support reuses the Class Power plumbing the Tank sheet introduces — a per-dwarf power + power_cd on the party dict, ticked host-side, fired by a validated tap intent. Communion is one new counter (communion on the party dict, +1 when a card that helps an ally resolves).

The Bard and Druid powers are stances (like Enrage), so they reuse that end-of-turn upkeep hook.

The Bard’s song is the cheapest thing on this sheet, and that’s not luck. Its rule — 3 distinct targets, AoE counts as 1 — needs no new addressing, because the host already validates every play as (target_kind, target_idx) in _try_play(seat, uid, kind, idx). That pair is the distinct-target key. Bank it in a per-turn targets set, check size() >= 3 at end of turn, and drop the song + set power_cd = 3 if not. “AoE counts as 1” is not a special case we write — it falls out: an AoE play carries a single address (kind="all", idx=-1), so it can only ever add one entry. It’s the same static address the M3b fx rider already ships as side+slot.

Wild Shape is the expensive one, and the cost is input, not logic: it needs two choices combat has never had to ask for. First a 3-way form pick on the tap (form = bear/hawk/wolf on the party dict, plus shift_turns = 3). Then, on the first shifted turn, a pick-2-from-hand modal to pay the tithe — a genuinely new input shape, since every existing play targets a character, not a card. Both are new validated intents in co-op (per-seat and instant — your own dwarf, so never a ring decision). The payout itself is trivial by comparison: at the start of each shifted turn, count the hand by type and multiply.

The Cleric’s Channel is passive — the simplest of the three: a per-cast hook tags each play 🔥 (attack) or 🙏 (skill/power), bumps a same-type streak (reset on switch) and a casts counter, and adds streak to the matching charge; on every 5th cast it fires a party heal + dmg_all sized by the two charges, then clears. No tap, no cooldown.

New ops: channel_aura (the Cleric per-cast hook: tag + streak + 5-cast discharge) · bank_communion v · spend_communion [ops] (scale by Communion, exactly like the shipped spend_devotion) · buff_ally_next v (Bard Inspiration) · the two stance powers (perform / wild_shape form) · shuffle_into_deck n (Forage + the Wild Shape filter) · hand_type_payout (the form’s start-of-turn count). Reused as-is: heal_ally, party_block, party_buff, shield_ally, dmg_all, apply/vulnerable+burn, temp/retaliate, draw, gain_guard. Everything else is pure data.

The one thing to sim first: the form payouts can’t be tuned in isolation — they’re a function of whatever list the Druid built, so they’re the only numbers on this sheet that move when a card changes. Bear at +5 per block card off a block-stacked deck is the obvious blow-out: a 3-block hand is +15 Guard for zero energy, every turn, for 3 turns — and you still get to play the blocks afterward. The −2 hand is the only brake, and there’s a real tension worth watching: the narrow deck that makes the payout wide is the same narrow deck that makes the tithe painless. If Wild Shape ends up dominant, that loop is why, and the lever is the cost (2 cards → 3) at least as much as the three payout numbers.
