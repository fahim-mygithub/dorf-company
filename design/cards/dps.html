Draft direction (2026-07-15). The DPS role, same three-layer shape as the Tank sheet: the role is a shared substrate, each class is one Class Power that runs on it, and a skill tree (post-MVP) upgrades that power. Numbers are unsimmed placeholders.

Rev 2026-07-17 — all three powers were rebuilt, and the substrate with them. Momentum is gone from this role. It measured a solo turn (+1 per attack you played, reset each turn), and not one of the three powers below is about a solo turn — so it was measuring the wrong thing. What replaces it isn’t another meter: it’s the rest of the party.

⚔️ DPS — your party charges your power

No personal meter. Every Class Power here is charged, extended or refunded by what your allies do:

🧙 Metamagic — charges when you or an ally casts a spell🗡️ Assassin’s Mark — lasts longer every time an ally hits it👊 Flurry — refunded every time anyone lands a status

None of the three is on a cooldown, and that’s the point: a cooldown counts turns, and these need to count teammates. It works because combat here is simultaneous — all three dwarves act in the same player phase, so your ally’s Burn lands while you still have your turn. The DPS role is where co-op actually pays out, and these powers are the payout: they’re the reason you shout “burn it!” at your friend instead of quietly optimising your own hand. Solo, all three still work — you just have to feed them all three dwarves yourself.

DRAFT — unsimmed

🧿 What a “spell” is — the one new piece of data

Two of these powers read spells, so the word has to mean something. It becomes a card’s school — one of three, on every card:

🛡️ block — it puts up a shield⚔️ physical — it swings something🧿 spell — everything else: a bolt, a heal, a mark, a bless

School is a second axis, not a rename of the type. The existing attack / skill / power type drives the tint and the targeting and doesn’t move. Strike and Bolt are both attack type — but Strike is physical and Bolt is a spell, and no amount of squinting at the existing type tells you that. Hence a tag.

It pays for itself immediately: the Druid’s three Wild Shape forms on the Support sheet already read exactly these three schools (🐻 Bear = block, 🐺 Wolf = physical, 🦅 Hawk = spell). One field, two sheets.

🟣 In combat: the Class Power

Each dwarf carries its Class Power as an orb on its portrait — a Hearthstone-style hero power. Tap your own dwarf to fire it; the orb shows the gate — and on this sheet the gate is never a cooldown. It reads what the party just did: spells cast, hits landed on your mark, statuses stuck. In co-op you only ever fire your own.

🌀 Twinned — the spell also hits a second target, in full.
⚡ Quicken — the spell costs 2 less (a 2-cost becomes free).
⏳ Heighten — the spell doesn’t fire now. At the start of your next turn it fires twice.
Three shapes, one choice, no maths: wider, cheaper, or later but double.

Heighten is the one to read twice — it is the only card in the game that deliberately does nothing on the turn you pay for it.

Name a target. It bleeds 4 a turn for 3 turns, and the bleed ignores armour completely — block, Guard, Bulwark, none of it matters. Then the fight feeds it, from both ends:
👥 Every ally hit on the marked target → +1 turn. Your party keeps it alive.

Neither half is worth much without the other: you alone build a vicious bleed that expires in 3 turns; the party alone keeps a 4-a-turn scratch running forever. Together the mark outlives the fight and gets worse every round it does.

Tap it and strike for 8. Then the cooldown lights up — and for the rest of this turn, every status effect applied to an enemy refunds it. Not just yours: a teammate’s Burn 🔥, the Sorcerer’s Mark 🎯, the Witch’s Hex — anyone’s. Refunded means tap it again.
So the Monk isn’t a card you play, it’s a rhythm you keep: status → Flurry → status → Flurry. The ceiling isn’t your energy or your hand — it’s how many statuses your party can land in one turn. Play beside a Cleric slinging Searing Word and a Rogue with Poison Blade and the fists simply don’t stop.

The build cost — what DPS adds

DPS was the lightest role — it scaled off Momentum, which already ships. Dropping Momentum gives that back: these three powers need hooks, and a hook is more work than a counter. The good news is that it’s the same three hooks, and once they exist all three powers are small.

The one new field: school (block / physical / spell) on every card def. It can’t be derived — Strike and Bolt are both attack type. Cheap, but it touches every card in card_db.gd, and the existing tags field is where it goes.

The three hooks, all host-side, all fired where the ops already resolve:
• on any card resolving → if school == spell, +1 to every Sorcerer’s meta_charge. Party-wide, so it reads the resolving SEAT, not the actor.
• on any attack landing → if the target carries an assassin_mark, +1 duration when the attacker is an ally, +2 tick when it’s the mark’s owner. The mark stores its owner seat; that’s the whole “both ends” rule.
• on any status applying to an enemy → clear power_cd for every Monk, if the Monk fired this turn. One line, and it is the entire Monk.

Ordering matters exactly once: Heighten must resolve at start of turn, before the hand is drawn, or a Quickened+Heightened spell can’t interact with the hand it was meant to set up.

New ops: metamagic (charge + the 3-way pick: twin / quicken / heighten) · assassin_mark (a DoT carrying owner + tick + duration) · flurry (flat hit + arm the refund window) · pierce_armour (the mark’s tick ignores block) · delayed_cast (Heighten’s held spell). Reused as-is: dmg, dmg_all, apply_status/marked, apply/vulnerable+burn, on_kill, gain_energy, draw, the whole status pipeline.

Co-op is free here, and that is not luck: all three hooks live on the host, which already resolves every seat’s plays in one place. The charge, the mark and the refund are just party-dict state, so they ride the existing absolute snapshot — a client never computes any of it, it renders it. The M3b fx rider already carries the animations.
